About Mars Aerospace Components

// The Business

Mars Aerospace Components is a make-to-order precision machining and fabrication shop specializing in titanium, Inconel, and aluminum aerospace structures. Founded in 2001 by former Raytheon engineers, Mars serves Tier 1 aerospace primes on both defense and commercial programs. Revenue is contract-driven with long cycle times — jobs run weeks to months, not days. Cost visibility at the job level is the critical reporting challenge.

// The Epicor Setup

Mars runs Epicor Kinetic with job costing, lot and serial traceability, and multi-level bill of materials. Their data spans three years of job history, contract billing milestones, and full lot genealogy across 85+ active part numbers. The Tailored.BI warehouse connects via the SQL Server ETL lane, using SysRevChangedDate watermark filtering. JobHead and LaborDtl are the highest-volume tables at over 2M rows combined.

For a manufacturer like Mars, Epicor Kinetic represents a $500K–$2M+ investment. Tailored.BI surfaces the value of that investment — turning job cost data into margin visibility that drives every contract decision.

Architecture Insight
Why Mars is a Different Epicor Problem
Same Tailored.BI warehouse architecture. Completely different data patterns. Mars demonstrates that the framework scales from simple make-to-stock all the way to complex make-to-order aerospace.
Job Costing vs Standard Cost
// Heartland = standard cost  |  Mars = job cost
Every Mars job has its own estimated vs actual cost variance. Reporting needs cost-to-complete, earned value, and labor efficiency at the job and operation level. Standard cost variance reports don't apply here.
Lot Traceability
// Heartland = no lot tracking  |  Mars = full genealogy
Every raw material lot is tracked from receiving through production to shipment. A dedicated bridge table maps lot numbers to jobs, customers, and certificates of conformance for AS9100D compliance reporting.
Contract Billing vs AR Aging
// Heartland = invoice aging  |  Mars = milestone billing
Mars bills against contract milestones, not shipments. Revenue recognition is tied to program completion percentage. AR aging is replaced by contract status and earned revenue dashboards.
Long Cycle Times
// Heartland = days  |  Mars = weeks to months
Jobs for structural components run 6-14 weeks. WIP inventory is substantial. On-time delivery requires comparing promised vs actual at the operation level, not just the order level.
